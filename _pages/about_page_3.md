# Automated Systems

This theme explores the use of automated systems, including traditional algorithms, deep learning, and large language models, to enhance diagnostics, predictive modeling, and decision-making in clinical and research settings.

## Traditional Systems

*Utilizes traditional machine learning algorithms to create predictive models for various clinical outcomes.*

1. **J. Thomas, C. Abdallah, et al.**, “Application of traditional machine learning models in predicting epilepsy outcomes,” *Computational Intelligence and Neuroscience*, 2023.
2. **J. Thomas, V. Koneru, P. Thangavel**, “Comparative analysis of SVM and random forest algorithms in neuroimaging data classification,” *Frontiers in Neuroscience*, 2022.

## Deep Learning Systems

*Focuses on using deep learning techniques, such as convolutional neural networks, to analyze complex neuroimaging data and enhance diagnostic accuracy.*

1. **J. Thomas, J. Jin, P. Thangavel, et al.**, “Deep learning approaches to automated epilepsy diagnosis using EEG data,” *Nature Communications*, 2021.
2. **J. Thomas, W. Y. Peh, et al.**, “Convolutional neural networks for the detection of abnormal brain activity,” *Neurocomputing*, 2020.

## Large Language Models (LLMs)

*Explores the application of LLMs in clinical settings to assist in diagnostics, patient communication, and data interpretation.*

1. **J. Thomas, G. Langs, V. Koneru**, “Implementing large language models for automated patient consultation,” *Journal of Medical Internet Research*, 2023.
2. **J. Thomas, P. Thangavel**, “Using LLMs for medical literature synthesis and review generation,” *Artificial Intelligence in Medicine*, 2024.
